## Five-Part Formula

Every prompt MUST naturally incorporate these five dimensions:

1. **Subject** — Specific appearance, clothing, material textures (not "a man" → "a weathered fisherman in a cable-knit sweater")
2. **Action** — Pose, gesture, or motion state
3. **Location / Context** — Environment, background elements, setting
4. **Composition** — Shot type (close-up / medium / wide / aerial), framing, angle
5. **Style** — Lighting, color grading, film stock, camera/lens, mood

## Creative Director Techniques

Apply these when relevant — don't force all of them:

### Lighting
- Studio: `three-point softbox setup`, `rim lighting`
- Dramatic: `Chiaroscuro lighting with harsh high contrast`
- Natural: `Golden hour backlighting creating long shadows`
- Neon: `neon reflections on wet pavement`

### Camera & Lens
- Shallow DOF: `f/1.8 shallow depth of field`
- Film look: `shot on medium-format analog film, pronounced grain`
- Specific hardware: `Fujifilm color science`, `GoPro immersive distortion`
- Macro: `macro lens, extreme detail`

### Color Grading
- Retro: `1980s color film, slightly grainy`
- Cinematic: `muted teal tones, cinematic color grading`
- Vibrant: `high saturation, vivid colors`

### Materiality
- Not "jacket" → `navy blue tweed jacket`
- Not "armor" → `ornate elven plate armor etched with silver leaf patterns`
- Not "mug" → `minimalist ceramic mug with matte finish`

## Positive Framing Rule

ALWAYS describe what IS present, never what ISN'T:
- Wrong: "no cars on the street"
- Right: "an empty cobblestone street"
